Exhibits

The Art of Coffee

Until 19 December, 2022

About this exhibit:

Coffee is more than a warm drink or a way to wake up in the morning. Coffee's rich cultural history has been painted across centuries and continents, from the warmth of the equator to the harshest northern climes.

The Art of Coffee exhibit draws together artwork that captures the beautiful and sometimes painful history of coffee, from its roots to your cup.

This exhibit is part of our Winter Wonderland seasonal festivities at the Nameless Museum of Fine Arts. Our café is serving a global selection of coffee preparations so you can enjoy the art, then warm up with a coffee!

Fruit and Flowers: A Retrospective

Until 2 July, 2023

About this exhibit:

Throughout history, both fruit and flowers have held prominent places in the mythology of humankind. From the earliest religious texts to modern text messages, they are symbols of life and death, plenty and poverty, beauty and loss.

Victorians invented a langauge based on different kinds of flowers. Economies have been built around the richness of fruit. They are entwined in our history, and of course, represented in our artwork.

The exhibit starts this winter and runs through spring and summer. Indulge in the colorful luxury and complex history of these beautiful plants.

From Sea to Sea to Sea: Canadian Landscapes

15 May - 20 August, 2022

About this exhibit:

Anyone who knows anything about Canada knows that it is almost the largest country, geographically speaking. Because most of the population lives along its southern border, much of the country retains the beauty of its natural state.

From Sea to Sea to Sea: Canadian Landscapes attempts to capture the bounty of the Canadian countryside. Featuring artists both well-known and obscure, this exhibit showcases Canadian landmarks like Niagara Falls. This exhibit celebrates Canada in all its grandeur, a vast expanse of natural features stretching from the Atlantic to the Pacific to the Arctic Oceans.

Awkward Moments Throughout History

21 October, 2021 - 20 April, 2022

About this exhibit:

We've all been there. One moment, you're socializing, enjoying a nice day at the park or a night out at the café, and the next you're plunged into a socially awkward situation worthy of your worst nightmares.

But these moments are nothing new. Awkward Moments Throughout History is an exhibit that demonstrates the perpetuity of social faux pas, ill-timed laughs, unfortunate turns of phrase, and uncomfortable realizations. Browsing among these painful scenes captured on canvas for posterity, you can comfort yourself with the knowledge that awkwardness is eternal.

Bowls

Starting August 2023

About this exhibit:

Along with the discovery of fire and the wheel, the creation of bowls for storage, serving, and eating marks one of the steps towards the development of civilisation as we know it. It is difficult to overstate the importance of these curved plates.

Across continents and civilizations with vastly different backgrounds, one thing common to all is the use of pottery, stone, or wood to create a curved vessel to store gathered food, serve it to community members, and display as objects of beauty.

This exhibit explores the history of bowls: their makers, the materials used and the environment that produced them, as well as the value of these objects and how it has changed over time.

Quilts

Starting February 2023

About this exhibit:

From the coziest flannel quilt made by grandma to the works of haute couture hung in art galleries, quilts are all the rage. This winter, the exhibit Quilts explores this artform, from its humble economical beginnings, through its complex mathematical composition, to its acknowledgement as a cultural treasure, both richly historic and cutting edge.

The quilts in this exhibit trace the arc from early Japanese examples, colonial American thrift, patchworks representing historical moments, and modern artworks fit to adorn the walls of palaces.

When you are feeling the cold bite of winter, consider a visit to our warm and cozy exhibit!

Rain

About this exhibit:

The cycle of water is never far from the thoughts of any creature on this planet. Essential for life on earth, water is a precious, constant companion, life-bringing and cleansing.

Water also has its downsides - or should we say, downpours! Torential rainstorms have played their part in the shaping of humankind. While rain is a gift that enables food to grow, it can have a fierce effect on the landscape, carving away mountainsides, flooding lowlands, and filling every crevice in city streets with puddles ripe to splash through.

The Rain exhibit celebrates falling water in all its beauty and agony. It's worth a visit, rain or shine!

Winter

About this exhibit:

Winter. Even the word exerts a chilling effect, a memory of icy toes and frozen nostrils. For those who like the bracing January air, winter is a delightful oppotunity to enjoy the outdoors. For those who prefer be cozy under a blanket with hot cocoa, unfortunately winter is impossible to avoid.

Canadian winter is world-famous for being harsh, long, and dark. But it is also known for its beauty, its pure snowfalls, and its peaceful evenings.

Our Winter exhibit puts the spotlight on the artistic side of the longest season, bringing attention to the colors to be found beyond the snow, and reminding us that even in the darkest hour, a beautiful winter sunrise is only fifteen hours away
